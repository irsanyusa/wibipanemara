5 romance novels that should be made into movies

June 15, 2024
blog

With the undefined of 'Fifty Shades of Grey', we have come up with a list of 5 romance books similar to E.L James' 'Fifty Shades of Grey' that should definitely be made into movies in future.

In no particular order, here we go;

ADVERTISEMENT

'Beautiful Bastard' is an erotic romance novel by Christina Hobbs and Lauren Billings under the singular pen name of Christina Lauren.

Whip-smart, hardworking, and on her way to an MBA, Chloe Mills has only one problem: her boss, Bennett Ryan. He's blunt, inconsiderate and totally irresistible.Bennett has returned to Chicago from France to take a vital role in his family's massive media business. He never expected that the assistant who'd been helping him from abroad to be gorgeous and innocently provocative.

Sylvia Day's Crossfire Series have sold more than 12 million copies worldwide. The most recent book in the series, Entwined With You, debuted at No. 1 on USA TODAY's Best-Selling Books list in June.

The series centers on Eva Tramell, a young woman in the New York advertising world, and her steamy relationship with billionaire businessman Gideon Cross.

ADVERTISEMENT

The 'Fixed Trilogy' is about Alayna Wither, a young lady with an obsessive love disorder.

With her MBA newly in hand, she has her future figured out and plans to stay away from any guy who might trigger her stalking disorder.

Until she meets Hudson Pierce, the new owner of the nightclub where she works. He’s smart, rich, and gorgeous—the kind of guy Alayna knows to stay away from except Hudson’s fixed his sights on her.

He wants her in his bed and makes no secret of it.

ADVERTISEMENT

'Just One Night' is about Kasie Fitzgerald, an ambitious workaholic who is devoted to the sensible, rational things in life. But one wild, impulsive night in Vegas opens her eyes to who she wants to be.

'Taking It All' is about Chessy and Tate who have been married for several years. In the beginning, their relationship was everything she wanted.

But as the years went by, Tate became more immersed in making his business a success, taking Chessy to the back of his priority list.

Growing unhappier with the status of their once blissful marriage, Chessy knows that something has to give or they stand to lose it all.
