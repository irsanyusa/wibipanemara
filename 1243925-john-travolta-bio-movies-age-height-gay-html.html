John Travolta is an American actor, producer, and dancer. He is probably best known for his role as Vincent Vega in the critically acclaimed 1994 movie Pulp Fiction. The actor has appeared in numerous films over his career that spans more than forty years. He has had his share of career highs, lows, awards, and controversies. How much do you know about this film veteran beyond his on-screen life?

John Travolta was born in New Jersey on 18th February 1954 to parents Helen and Salvatore. John Travolta age is currently 65 years. His mum was an actress of Irish descent while his father was a semi-professional footballer. Later his father became a tire salesman and a partner in a tire manufacturing firm. John grew up alongside five siblings, namely: Joe, Margaret, Sam, Ellen, and Ann. The actor attended Dwight Morrow High School but dropped out at the age of 17.

John Travolta movies

The actor got his big-screen break in 1975 when he appeared in the movie The Devil’s Rain and later in Carrie (1976). The actor then got his break into the international arena when he was cast as Tony Manero in the 1977 film Saturday Night Fever. The movie followed Tony, a paint store clerk who joins a dancing competition and convinces the beautiful Stephanie (Karen Lynn) to be his dance partner. As the pair trains for the competition, they begin to fall for each other. Travolta was nominated for various awards for his role in the movie.

After the box office success of Saturday Night Fever, John was named in the cast for the 1978 movie musical Grease. The film followed two teenagers, Danny and Sandy who fall in love during summer vacation. Travolta played the role of Danny. The film was very successful due to its massive audience. The movie raked in around $400 million and had a $6 million production budget. With those figures, Grease became one of the highest-grossing action movie musicals.

After his run of successful projects, Travolta’s acting career began to decline with a series of unpopular films. Top among the poor-performing was the Saturday Night Fever 1983 sequel Staying Alive. This was followed by the equally poor Two of a Kind (1983) and Perfect (1985). The actor enjoyed brief success with the Look Who’s Talking series alongside Kirstie Alley. However, by the end of the 1980s, Travolta was in the ‘has-been’ category of actors.

After a significant decline in his acting career, John made an epic comeback in 1994 when he starred in the film Pulp Fiction. The movie was directed by Quentin Tarantino and followed two hit men, Jules Winnfield (Samuel L Jackson) and Vincent Vega (Travolta). The film earned Travolta an Academy Award nomination. A scene where he dances with Uma Thurman is also on the list of the favourite film scenes of all time. After Pulp Fiction, John was back on the acting limelight.

In 1995, the actor played the character Chill in the comedy Get Shorty. His performance in the novel-based movie won him a Golden Globe award. A sequel to Get Shorty was released in 2005 titled Be Cool but was not as successful as the first movie.

Within the 90s decade, the actor appeared in films such as Primary Colors (1998), Phenomenon (1996) and Arrow (1996). John’s final film in the 90s was The General’s Daughter (1999). The beginning of the following decade was not very kind to the actor. His first movie of the decade Battlefield Earth (2000) was a massive miss. The film was based on a novel by Ron Hubbard, the Scientology founder. Despite the flop, the actor was back again, this time in the movie Swordfish (2001) alongside Hugh Jackman and Halle Berry. Other notable Travolta movies within that period included Wild Hogs, Ladder 49 and Love Song for Bobby Long.

John Travolta gay rumours

What is perhaps the most significant controversy surrounding the actor concerns allegations of being gay. In 2006, the actor was alleged to have kissed a male colleague before boarding his private plane in Canada. In 2012, two men intended to sue the actor for emotional distress and sexual battery. Travolta’s former pilot (Douglas Gotterba) also claimed that he and the actor were involved in a relationship in the 1980s.

However, Travolta dismissed all these allegations in 2014 and claimed them to be made up by people who wanted money from him. He termed the gay rumours as ‘every actor’s Achilles heel’. Travolta’s attorney (Marty Singer) also dismissed the claims. He claimed that his client’s accusers were looking for their fifteen minutes of fame by fabricating stories about things that supposedly took place.

New Jersey

The John Travolta house in New Jersey was his first home purchase. He bought it for a paltry $140,000 and resided in it for five years. Later, he gave it to his sister when his career life began to rise. The home is now valued at $290,000.

Santa Barbara

The Travolta family owns homes on the country’s East and West coasts. They purchased the Santa Barbara house for about $7 million back in 1997. The home is equipped with six fireplaces, a swimming pool, and a tennis court. According to the family, they rarely spend time in California, but when they do, it is always at their Santa Barbara home.

Ocala, Florida

The Ocala residence is the family’s primary home. The actor purchased it from Frank Sinatra for $3.5 million. He then renovated the estate to accommodate his family as well as his collection of private planes. The home is part of an exclusive area for the aviation community known as Jumbolair Estates. There are roadways leading to the front of each home and plane taxiways leading to the back.
