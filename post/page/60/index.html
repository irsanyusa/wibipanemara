TIME January 3, 1955 12:00 AM GMT-5 In their mustard-colored uniforms and jaunty green berets, the Binh Xuyen of Saigon are probably the most exotic mob of hoodlums in Asia. Four thousand strong, they are the shock troops of the Saigon police, an empire of their own within the South Viet Nam state. Each day the Binh Xuyen (pronounced bin soo yen) pays the Vietnamese government about $10,000 in “taxes,” and gets in return the monopoly control of Saigon’s brothels, gambling casinos and opium dens (which are called “clinics of disintoxication”).
